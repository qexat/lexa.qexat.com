The potting soil

November 21st, 2025

I have always been an observer of the world. Silent, my eyes fixed on a person or object in my surroundings, trying to imagine what could have led the object of my contemplation to be in that precise place. The only thing that always eluded me was the glimmer on the horizon. In the biting cold of winter, sitting on the bench, I dreamed of what I already saw. Regularly, a pang of longing would arise from my desire to be on the other side of this world. It would have been a lie to say that I didn't want to be the one being observed. However, my choices and my constraints led me to this place, imprisoned by loneliness and the conviction that I couldn't escape.

I don't know if I'm too young to have so many regrets. I know others with wounds just as deep, if not deeper. In the past, I thought complaining was something I had to earn, and that I had to suffer quite a bit to be able to do it. When the moment finally came in my life when I realized the absurdity of this belief that had gripped me all these years, I imagined I could sweep it aside and start doing things right.

Time is something ignoble. Impartial. Often, I wish it would pass quickly, but more critically, I wish it would slow down. It seems so uncontrollable, even though it's only one axis of perception of the incredibly complex world around me. Very often, I am my own prison. But there are certain shackles that free me from punishments that only disappear when I accept that I will suffer them for eternity. For all these days, so few and so many, bad, delicious, mediocre, profoundly boring, exciting, which will flow over my powerless and finite body which will give back to the earth the nutrients it entrusted to me.
